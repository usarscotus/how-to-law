    <h3 className="text-lg font-semibold text-foreground">Daubert v. Merrell Dow Pharmaceuticals, Inc. (1993)</h3>
    <p className="mt-3 text-sm text-foreground/90">Federal judges must act as gatekeepers by ensuring that expert testimony rests on reliable principles and methods.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-sm text-foreground/90">Rule 702 requires courts to assess scientific validity, including testing, peer review, error rates, and general acceptance, before admitting expert evidence.</p>

    <h3 className="text-lg font-semibold text-foreground">Crawford v. Washington (2004)</h3>
    <p className="mt-3 text-sm text-foreground/90">Admitting testimonial statements from an absent witness violates the Sixth Amendment unless the defendant had a prior opportunity for cross-examination.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-sm text-foreground/90">The Confrontation Clause bars testimonial hearsay unless the declarant is unavailable and the accused had a chance to cross-examine.</p>

    <h3 className="text-lg font-semibold text-foreground">Huddleston v. United States (1988)</h3>
    <p className="mt-3 text-sm text-foreground/90">Evidence of prior acts is admissible under Rule 404(b) if the jury could reasonably find by a preponderance that the acts occurred and the evidence is relevant for a non-propensity purpose.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-sm text-foreground/90">Judges conduct a Rule 104(b) conditional relevance inquiry without weighing credibility, and must still balance probative value against unfair prejudice under Rule 403.</p>

    <h3 className="text-lg font-semibold text-foreground">Old Chief v. United States (1997)</h3>
    <p className="mt-3 text-sm text-foreground/90">A court abuses its discretion by admitting the full record of a prior conviction when the defendant offers to stipulate to the status element and the record's unfair prejudice outweighs its probative value.</p>
    <p className="mt-2 text-sm text-foreground/90">Rule 403 requires comparing evidentiary alternatives; when a stipulation satisfies an element with minimal prejudice, the prosecution must accept it.</p>
